# Domain 3: Information Security Program Development and Management

✅ **Main Focus**  
Domain 3 accounts for **33% of the CISM exam** and covers:  

- Information Security Program Development  
- Information Security Program Management  

---

## 1. Purpose of an Information Security Program

- Security does not exist in isolation; it must be managed as a program.  
- Requires a **top-down approach** with executive support.  
- Aligns with **business goals, risk tolerance, and organizational culture**.  

---

## 2. Program Charter

A **formal document** defining:  

- Objectives  
- Timelines  
- Funding sources  
- Leadership roles  

**Key points:**  
- Approved by CEO or executive leadership.  
- Gives authority to security leaders for:  
  - Policy development  
  - Risk management  
  - Governance  
  - Control implementation  
  - Incident response  
  - Business continuity planning  

---

## 3. Scope of the Program

- Defines boundaries: departments, units, affiliates, locations.  
- Ensures clarity on what is covered under **security governance**.  

---

## 4. Core Components

1. **Processes:** Risk & compliance, architecture, operations  
2. **Technologies:** Firewalls, IDS/IPS, DLP, anti-malware, GRC platforms  
3. **Assets:** Hardware, software, data, facilities, personnel  

---

## 5. Information & System Classification

- Based on:  
  - Monetary value  
  - Operational criticality  
  - Integrity  
  - Sensitivity  
  - Reputation impact  
- Example levels: Secret, Restricted, Confidential, Public  
- Apply **system hardening standards** for each classification  

---

## 6. Frameworks & Standards

- **Control Frameworks:** ISO 27002, NIST SP 800-53, CIS CSC  
- **Risk Management Frameworks:** ISO 31000, NIST CSF  
- **Program Management Frameworks:** ISO 27001, ETSI TR 103787  

---

## 7. Policy Development

- Align with laws, regulations, risk tolerance, and organizational culture  
- Must be **clear, accessible, and relevant** to all employees  

---

## 8. Security Metrics

- Types:  
  - **KRIs** (Key Risk Indicators)  
  - **KGIs** (Key Goal Indicators)  
  - **KPIs** (Key Performance Indicators)  
- Use **SMART criteria:** Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Timely  

---

## 9. Controls

- **Types:** Physical, Technical, Administrative  
- **Classes:** Preventive, Detective, Deterrent, Corrective, Compensating, Recovery  
- **Categories:** Automatic vs. Manual  
- **Lifecycle:** Design → Test → Implement → Monitor → Audit  

---

## 10. Operational Activities

- Event monitoring, log reviews, SIEM, threat intelligence, SOAR  
- Vulnerability management, patch management  
- Endpoint protection, anti-malware, EDR/XDR  
- Identity & Access Management (IAM), MFA, passwordless authentication  
- Access governance, segregation of duties, access reviews  

---

## 11. Data Governance

- Backup & recovery strategies  
- Data classification policies  
- Data Loss Prevention (DLP) systems  
- Digital Rights Management (DRM)
